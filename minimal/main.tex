\section{Variable}
\textit{Variable} in CGE model must be defined as percentage-change or linearized variables. We use lowercase to identify these kind of variables. To update variables, we use syntax \textit{EQUATION}. 

\subsection{Linearized patterns}
Note that
\begin{equation}
    y=\frac{dY}{Y}\times100
\end{equation}
Mathematics of change
\begin{equation}
    dY=\frac{Yy}{100}
\end{equation}
and
\begin{align}
    \frac{d\log Y}{dY} &= \frac{1}{Y} \\
    \frac{dY}{Y}\times 100 &= d\log Y\times100 \\
    y &= d\log Y\times100 \\
\end{align}
To convert level form into linearized form, we apply total differential
\begin{equation}
    z=f(x,y) \Longrightarrow dz= f'_1(x,y)dx+f'_2(x,y)dy
\end{equation}
Example:
\begin{align}
    Y = X^2+Z \Longrightarrow dY=2XdX+dZ
\end{align}
Recall that $dy = \dfrac{Yy}{100}$. Hence,
\begin{align}
    Y = X^2+Z \Longrightarrow dY &= 2XdX+dZ \\
    \frac{Yy}{100} &= 2X\frac{Xx}{100} + \frac{Zz}{100} \\
    Yy &= 2XXx + Zz \\
\end{align}

Proof
Linearized $Y=X+Z$
Apply differential and get
\begin{align}
    dY &= dX + dZ \\
    Yy &= Xx + Zz 
\end{align}
